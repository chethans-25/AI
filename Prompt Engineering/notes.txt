The 4 Elements of a "A Good Prompt"

1. Instruction: Suggest a dinner recipe I can make
2. Context: I am a Vegetarian and prefer meals that take less than an hour.
3. Input Data: I have Rice, Tomato, Onions, spices and Carrots.
4. Output Indicator: please list the ingredients and provide step-by-step instructions.

Templates
Some apps include prompt templates to standardize input, ensuring consistency and reusability.

**************************************************
Prompt Engineering Techniques
1. Zero Shot
Uses no example, rely entirely on model's knowledge. Suitable for straightforward tasks.
e.g. Suggest a beginner exercise for weight loss

2. One Shot
Provide 1 example to set expectation.
e.g. What year was gravity discovered? 1665 AD
What year was electricity discovered?
Response: 600 BC

3. Few Shot
Offer Multiple examples to teach pattern, and expect output in similar pattern

4. Chain of thought
Guide the model to break down reasoning into logical steps, improving performance on tasks like math problem or troubleshooting.

5. Negative
Instruct the model on what to avoid, reducing the risk of undesirable output.

Inference Parameters for Gen AI

Inference:
"I hear the hoof beats of .............."
 
                      [horses, zebras, donkeys, girragges, unicorns, ...]
Initial Probability   [70,      20,     5,        2,          1    , ...]
<Randomness and Diversity>
Temperature effect:   [60,      15,     10,       3,          3    , ...] // temp is high ( say 1 )-> flattens the distribution
Top P: say 0.85, top 85% will be chosen; (either of these: horses, zebras, donkeys)
Top K: say 60, top 60 values will be considered.
Top P and Top K work together.

<Length>
Max Length: specifies the Max Length of generated response by limiting the number of tokens.
Stop Sequences: Defines specific sequence of characters or tokens that signal the model to stop generating further.
